Difference Between Trademark and a Copyright

Intellectual property is safeguarded by copyrights and registered trademarks, each having a unique function.

Copyright

Protect Your Creative Works

Copyrights protect creative works such as stories, poems, songs, movies, and software code. They ensure that the rights of creators are respected, and their efforts are acknowledged.

Legal Ownership

Creating original content provides legal protection under copyright law. Our team of experts ensures your hard work is safeguarded against infringement. Protect your brand today!

Long Expiry Span

Did you know that a person's copyright lasts their entire lifetime plus 70 years? However, there are separate rules for works created anonymously or under a pseudonym.

Trademark

Intellectual Property Protection

Trademark is an essential tool for the protection of brands, logos, slogans, and product names, establishing recognition and identity.

Filing with the USPTO

Securing a trademark with the USPTO ensures comprehensive and enforceable legal rights, offering better protection than just using your name and logo.

Renew After 5-10 Years

Trademarks are initially registered for a specific duration, typically lasting 10 years. However, the exact duration may vary from country to country.

Reasons Why Your Trademark Gets Rejected

To ensure a smooth trademark registration process, it is important to be aware of potential obstacles that may cause delays. Here are some key points to consider:

  • Trademarks are used to protect brands in commerce and cannot be registered for personal or non-business use.
  • Trademark names should be unique and distinct, not generic or descriptive, to set the brand apart from competitors.
  • You can only register a trademark for a name that is currently being used in business or one that you plan to use in the near future.
  • If your proposed name closely resembles an existing registered or pending trademark, it may lead to mistaken assumptions about the source of goods or services.
